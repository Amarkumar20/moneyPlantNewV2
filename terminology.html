Use this quick guide for a successful and smooth purchase plan that is given below:

  • Establish a budget, considering all relevant costs and fees, such as a 25% deposit. Pay some attention to the exchange rate, too, because it could fluctuate anytime if the US Federal Reserve makes any changes that could affect the UAE dirham's value.

  • Know your mortgage eligibility with an advisor who can help you understand mortgage products properly and provide approval procedures. This will secure your dream home for you.

  • Hire a skilled broker who will make you meet with the key stakeholders and support you during negotiations.

  • Submit your offer through your broker and ensure it is confirmed and secured by a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU). This critical document covers the details and terms of the purchase.

  • Go through the sales agreement very carefully. And make sure that you understand all points, then agree with all the terms and conditions that are written in the sales agreement.

  • To ensure the property has been paid for in full and is free of any outstanding charges or maintenance fees, your broker will apply for the developer's No Objection Certificate (NOC). It could take up to seven business days. You will receive your new title deed and be able to move into your new home once you have completed the transfer of property ownership and registered the sale with the Dubai Land Department.
